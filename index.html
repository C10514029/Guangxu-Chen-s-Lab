Welcome to Guangxu Chen's Research Group

We are a materials chemistry research group at South China University of Technology (华南理工大学), dedicated to the fundamental study of precise and efficient conversion of small molecules related to energy and environmental applications.

Our research combines controlled nanomaterial synthesis, advanced structural characterization, and mechanistic studies to develop next-generation electro- and thermocatalysts for sustainable chemistry.

Dr. Guangxu Chen is a Professor and Ph.D. advisor at South China University of Technology and a recipient of the National Overseas Young Talent Program (2018). He earned his Ph.D. under the supervision of Academician Prof. Nanfeng Zheng at Xiamen University and conducted postdoctoral research with Academician Prof. Yi Cui at Stanford University.

His research focuses on the fundamental study of precise and efficient conversion of small molecules related to energy and environmental applications, including the controlled synthesis of functional nanomaterials, structural characterization, electro-/thermocatalytic performance, reaction mechanisms, and the design and development of catalytic reaction systems.

He has led multiple research projects including the National Overseas Young Talent Program, a key project under the Ministry of Science and Technology's Key R&D Program on Circular Economy, two NSFC general projects, a Ministry of Science and Technology foreign expert project, and two Guangdong "Pearl River Talent Plan" projects.

He has published over 60 SCI papers in journals including Science, Nature Materials, Nature Catalysis, Nature Water, Nature Communications (4), JACS, ACS Catalysis (2), Nano Letters (2), ACS Nano (5), and others. His work has received over 16,153 citations, with 13 ESI Highly Cited Papers and an H-index of 49.

He was awarded the 2018 National Natural Science Award (Second Prize) — "Coordination Chemistry of Metal Nanomaterials" (R4). He has applied for 25 patents (10 granted) and 1 PCT patent, with 2 technology transfers completed.

Research

We focus on the fundamental study of precise and efficient conversion of energy- and environment-related small molecules (CO₂, N₂, H₂O, organic compounds, plastics). We combine controlled nanomaterial synthesis, advanced in-situ characterization, and mechanistic studies to develop next-generation catalysts.

⚡

Electrocatalysis

Electrochemical Precise Conversion of Alcohols and Aldehydes: Electrochemical oxidation and reduction of biomass-derived compounds (HMF, benzyl alcohol, glycerol) using tailored metal interfaces for sustainable chemical production.

Electrochemical CO₂ Fixation and Direct Utilization: Design of Cu-based catalysts for selective CO₂ electroreduction to C2+ products, integrated with wastewater denitrification and CO₂ capture systems.

🔋

Electrochemical Recovery & Separation

Electrodialysis-Based Systems: Development of electrochemical recovery and separation systems grounded in electrodialysis principles for efficient ion and product recovery.

Expanded Applications: Electrochemical synthesis and separation, alkali recovery from waste streams, and integrated multi-functional electrochemical platforms.

♻️

Resource Recycling & Upcycling

Catalytic conversion of plastic waste (polyethylene, polystyrene, PET) into valuable chemicals via thermal and electrochemical approaches. Developing sustainable circular-economy pathways for carbon-containing waste streams.

🤖

AI + Automated Synthesis

Development of autonomous robotic platforms and AI-assisted approaches for end-to-end nanoparticle synthesis, high-throughput characterization, and intelligent catalyst screening.
